The Technology

Harnessing Light to
Eliminate Pathogens

Antimicrobial photodynamic therapy (aPDT) leverages the intersection of a photosensitizing compound, molecular oxygen, and targeted visible light to generate reactive oxygen species that selectively destroy microbial targets — without inducing resistance.

01
SCT-101 Photosensitizer
A proprietary lipophilic cationic phenothiazine photosensitizer with optimized membrane penetration, singlet oxygen quantum yield, and photophysical properties specifically tailored for topical antimicrobial applications.
Active Pharmaceutical Ingredient
02
670 nm Red-Light Device
A compact, purpose-built LED panel delivering 670 nm red light at ~100 mW/cm² — precisely matched to the absorption peak of SCT-101. Analog-controlled, software-free design minimizes regulatory complexity and enables robust field use.
Drug–Device Combination
03
Topical Formulation
Optimized gel vehicle with permeation enhancers designed for localized cutaneous delivery. Formulation development in collaboration with specialist pharmaceutical manufacturers, with stability and bioavailability studies underway.
Topical / Local Application
How It Works

A Precisely Orchestrated
Photochemical Cascade

ROS
1
Topical Application
SCT-101 formulation is applied directly to the target lesion site. The lipophilic cationic structure facilitates selective uptake into microbial membranes while limiting systemic absorption.
2
Photon Absorption
670 nm red light is delivered to the treatment site. SCT-101 absorbs photons at its peak absorption wavelength, exciting the molecule to a triplet state without tissue heating.
3
Reactive Oxygen Generation
Energy transfer to molecular oxygen generates singlet oxygen and other reactive oxygen species via Type I and Type II photochemical pathways — highly cytotoxic to microbial targets.
4
Multi-Target Microbial Destruction
ROS simultaneously damage microbial cell membranes, proteins, and nucleic acids through multiple independent mechanisms — precluding the development of resistance and eliminating established biofilms.
Why aPDT

Properties That
Conventional Therapy Cannot Match

Zero Resistance Liability
No resistance to aPDT has been reported across decades of published preclinical and clinical literature. The multi-target mechanism of ROS-mediated killing cannot be overcome by single or multi-gene mutations.
Broad-Spectrum Efficacy
Effective against Gram-positive and Gram-negative bacteria, fungi, protozoa, and established biofilms. SCT-101 demonstrates activity across clinically relevant resistant organisms including MRSA and carbapenem-resistant strains.
Localized Action
Light-dependent activation confines therapeutic effect to the illuminated treatment zone. Minimal systemic exposure with topical administration, preserving the microbiome and avoiding off-target toxicity.
Accessible Delivery Platform
Simple topical gel plus compact LED device — no specialized infrastructure required. Designed for outpatient clinical settings including resource-constrained environments where infectious skin diseases are most prevalent.
